4(2).Director of Communication and Engagement

   The Director of Communication and Engagement at a business university holds a key leadership position dedicated to shaping and implementing communication strategies that enhance the institution's image, engage diverse stakeholders, and foster a sense of community. This multifaceted role plays a crucial part in establishing and maintaining positive relationships both within the university and with external partners.

Responsibilities:

Strategic Communication Planning:

   The Director formulates and executes strategic communication plans aligned with the university's mission and goals. This involves collaborating with senior leadership to ensure consistency and effectiveness in messaging.


Brand Management:

   Managing the university's brand is a core responsibility. The Director ensures a unified and positive brand image by overseeing the consistent application of brand guidelines across various communication channels.


Media Relations:

   Serving as the primary liaison with media outlets, the Director manages media relations by responding to inquiries, preparing press releases, and cultivating positive relationships with journalists to promote the university's achievements and initiatives.


Internal Communication:

   The Director fosters effective internal communication by developing channels and strategies to keep faculty, staff, and students informed about university news, policies, and events. This includes regular newsletters, intranet updates, and town hall meetings.


Publications and Content Creation:

   Overseeing the creation of compelling content, the Director manages publications such as newsletters, magazines, and digital content. This content showcases the university's research, achievements, and contributions to the business community.


Digital and Social Media Management:

   Developing and implementing a comprehensive digital and social media strategy, the Director leverages various online platforms to engage with the university community, share updates, and enhance the institution's online presence.


Crisis Communication:

   The Director is responsible for developing and implementing crisis communication plans. In times of challenges or crises, they serve as a spokesperson, ensuring transparent and effective communication to protect the university's reputation.

Qualifications:

  • A bachelor's or master's degree in communications, public relations, marketing, or a related field.
  • Proven experience in strategic communication and engagement roles, preferably in higher education.
  • Strong interpersonal and leadership skills.
  • Excellent written and verbal communication abilities.
  • Proficiency in digital marketing, social media platforms, and content creation.
  • Experience in crisis communication and issues management.
