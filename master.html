Master Degree Program

M.Sc (Finance and Accounting)

MSc in Accounting and Finance (Financial Analysis Focus):

- Prepares for careers in financial analysis, accounting, and finance globally.

- Provides deep understanding of accounting, finance, and financial analysis, along with analytical skills.

- Equips with professional attributes and specialized financial analytical skills for business decision-making.

- Suitable for graduates with strong numeracy skills, regardless of background.

- Covers relevant material for Chartered Financial Analyst (CFA) exams.

- On-campus program in Newcastle upon Tyne, UK, offered in English.

- Duration: 12-24 months, full-time pace.

- Tuition fees vary for international and home students, with part-time options available.

- Next postgraduate virtual open day scheduled for May 17th.

M.Sc (Business Administration)

- Designed for students aiming for careers in business and finance sectors.

- Offers practical, fully accredited, and professionally oriented education.

- Provides high-level skills, knowledge, and expertise for chartered accountant or corporate services roles.

- Offers exemptions from ACCA and CIMA exams via the Masters' Gateway, an accelerated entry route.

- Aligned with Strategic Professional Level syllabus of ACCA qualification.

- Focuses on financial management areas with optional modules.

- Equips students with technical, professional, analytical, and ethical skills for global accounting careers.

- Utilizes Bloomberg data in the Financial Markets Suite for real-world scenario evaluation and critical thinking enhancement.

- Considers applicants with degrees in accounting, finance, economics, mathematics, business administration, or commerce.

- Salaries for graduates can be above average, with roles like accounting controllers and financial managers earning $103,560 and $131,710 annually, respectively.

M.Sc (Management Information Systems)

MBA graduates possess a broad skill set suitable for various career paths, with a considerable percentage leveraging their degree to transition into new fields, according to a survey by the Graduate Management Admissions Council. Common roles for MBA holders encompass business analysis, strategy, and development, alongside market research and management positions such as managing director. Opportunities in investment banking, entrepreneurship, and financial analysis are also prevalent. Management consulting offers a platform for applying strategic and analytical skills across diverse industries. In marketing, roles like marketing associate, analyst, or manager are accessible, while healthcare administration positions are viable options. Other potential paths include project, product, and operations management, highlighting the adaptability and versatility of an MBA education. Median advertised salaries range from $85,200 for Training and Development Managers to $105,200 for Compensation and Benefits Managers, reflecting significant earning potential for MBA graduates.

M.A (Marketing)

Master of Marketing: - Equips graduates with skills in strategy, branding, and market analysis.

- Opens doors to roles in sales, advertising, and brand management across industries.

- Curriculum blends theory and practical experiences, including digital marketing and elective modules like luxury management or data analytics.

- Graduates secure positions such as marketing analysts, coordinators, managers, and directors.

- Average salaries range from US$45,000 to over US$175,000, depending on experience and location.

- Notable programs offered by institutions like HEC Paris and Columbia Business School.

- Graduates can apply expertise in diverse fields beyond traditional marketing roles.

- Valuable assets in today's job market include understanding data, psychology, and economics.
